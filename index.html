The real problem

It isn't output length. It's confidence.

A hard max_steps cap is blunt: it kills good tasks at step 14, and still lets confident-but-wrong loops burn budget before the cap trips. Gate watches whether the model agrees with itself across samples — and acts on that.

FAQ

Straight answers.

Isn't this just a max_steps cap?

No. A cap fires on a fixed count — it cuts good tasks early and lets confident-but-wrong loops burn budget first. Gate fires on measured uncertainty (sample disagreement), so it acts on the agent that's actually guessing, not the one that happens to be on step 15.

How is "uncertainty" measured?

Disagreement across samples — when the model gives inconsistent answers to the same step, it's guessing. We measured AUC 0.71–0.91 across 16 model families. It's not a heuristic about length; it's a signal about the model's own confidence, and it's model-agnostic (no training).

Will it slow my agent down?

On confident steps Gate passes through with minimal overhead. The sampling cost only kicks in where it pays off — exactly the steps where a runaway loop would otherwise cost you dollars.

What does it work with?

Anything OpenAI-compatible — Aider, OpenHands, SWE-agent, or your own loop. You change base_url, nothing else. The open-core engine runs on your own model too.
